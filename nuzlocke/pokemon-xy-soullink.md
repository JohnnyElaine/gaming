# Pokémon X/Y Multi-Player SoulLink Hardcore Nuzlocke Ruleset

## Core Rules (Standard Hardcore Nuzlocke)

- **First Encounter Only:** On each new route / area, each player may only catch the first Pokémon they encounter (random or static encounters count).

- **Route/Area ID:** The area specified in the Pokémons detail page dictates the area it was caught in.

- **Dupes Clause:** If the first encounter is a species a player already owns (alive or dead), they must skip it for the next unique species. Works per player independently.

- **Fainting = Death:** Any Pokémon that faints is considered dead and must be permanently boxed or released. It cannot be used again.

- **Nickname All Pokémon:** All caught Pokémon must be nicknamed. Encounters across Players must have the same nickname. *e.g. all players catch a pokemon on route 1 and must give it the same name, for example "John Smith"*

- **No active Items in Battle:** You cannot use any items (Potions, Revives, X-items, etc.) during battle. Held items are allowed.  
   *Outside of battle, items are fine.*

- **Set Battle Mode:** You must play on "Set" battle style.

- **Level Caps** Pokémon levels may never exceed the level cap of the **next major boss fight** (Gym Leader, Team Flare boss, Elite Four, Champion). If a Pokémon exceeds the cap before the fight, it cannot be used until the cap rises. See [Level Caps]() below
   - **Exception:** Pokémon that level up *during* a boss fight to exactly the cap or one above are allowed to continue the fight, but cannot exceed the cap before the fight starts.

## SoulLink Rules

- **Encounter-Link:** Encounters for players on each route are linked. If one player fails to catch their encounter for a route, the other player must box or release their encounter for that route as well. If both players successfully catch their first encounter for the route, those two Pokémon are “linked” as partners.
- **Name-Link:** Linked Pokémon must have the same name.
-  **Death-Link:** When one player’s Pokémon faints, its partner on the other player’s team must be boxed or released as well. The death is applied after the each battle. *e.g. if all players face a trainer and Pokémon 1 only dies for trainer 1, the other trains may still use Pokémon 1 until the end of this battle*.
- **Box-Link:** If a Pokémon is placed in the PC, its partner on the other player’s team must be placed in the PC as well.
- **Party-Link:** If a Pokémon is placed in the Party, its linked-partner on the other player’s team must be placed in the Party as well.

## Victory / Loss Conditions

- **Victory:** All three players defeat the Champion with at least one surviving Trio-Link (or at least one Pokémon each) following all rules.
- **Loss:** Any player's entire party dies (all 6 dead) → full game reset for all three. *i.e. if any battle is lost*. Or if any player is out of useable Pokémon.
